## Content Strategy Notes

### What Ranks in This Space
- **Mega-lists win**: Posts with 40-100+ items consistently outrank shorter lists
- **Categorization matters**: Grouping by cost, time, skill level, or social context improves UX and SEO
- **Personal experience adds credibility**: First-person "I tried this" sections outperform generic descriptions
- **Interactive elements differentiate**: Quizzes, filters, and "find your hobby" tools are rare -- this is where SignificantHobbies has a natural advantage

### Differentiation Opportunities for SignificantHobbies
1. **Quiz integration**: Every blog post can end with "Not sure which to try? Take our hobby quiz" -- this is the core product advantage
2. **User stories**: Pull real user profiles/hobby journeys from the platform
3. **Community data**: "Our users' most popular hobbies in [category]" is unique data no competitor has
4. **Progress tracking**: Blog posts can link to hobby tracking features on the platform
5. **Cost/time/space filters**: Build interactive filters into list posts rather than static text

### Keyword Gaps (Low Competition, High Opportunity)
These keywords have very few quality results and represent the best quick-win opportunities:
- "5-minute hobbies" -- almost no one targets this
- "hobbies for hot weather" -- underserved
- "hobbies after kids" -- very few dedicated posts
- "snow day hobbies for adults" -- mostly kid content
- "hobbies for extroverts" -- introvert content is 10x more common
- "apartment hobbies" -- urban living niche is growing
- "hobbies without internet" -- aligned with the massive digital detox trend
- "lunch break hobbies" -- micro-hobby concept is novel

### Cross-Linking Strategy
Every blog post should link to:
1. The hobby quiz (primary CTA)
2. 2-3 related blog posts from this content calendar
3. Relevant hobby profile pages on the platform
4. The explore page with appropriate category filters applied
