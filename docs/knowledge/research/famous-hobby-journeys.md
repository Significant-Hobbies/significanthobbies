## 1. Bill Gates

**Known for:** Co-founder of Microsoft, philanthropist, one of the wealthiest people in history

**Hobbies Across Life Phases:**
- **Childhood:** Voracious reader -- read the entire World Book encyclopedia set by age 8. Loved math, strategy board games, and chess. His elementary school librarian introduced him to biographies of famous figures.
- **Teens/Early Career:** Became obsessed with computers and programming at Lakeside School. Spent hours coding and would "disappear into his room and lose myself in books or ideas, often for hours without interruption."
- **Career Peak:** Instituted "Think Weeks" -- spending entire weeks alone in a Pacific Northwest cabin reading papers and thinking. Read a book a week on average. Played bridge with Warren Buffett.
- **Later Life:** Plays Settlers of Catan with his family. Avid tennis player. Continues reading voraciously (mostly nonfiction about history, science, technology, and economics). Tracks AI and robotics developments as both professional and personal interest.

**Surprising Hobbies:**
- Plays bridge competitively (learned from his parents, deepened through friendship with Warren Buffett)
- Settlers of Catan family game nights
- "Think Weeks" in isolation -- a structured hobby of pure reading and thinking

**How Hobbies Influenced Career:**
- His childhood habit of deep, uninterrupted reading and thinking became the foundation of his analytical approach to business and philanthropy
- Think Weeks at Microsoft led to major strategic pivots, including the famous "Internet Tidal Wave" memo
- Travel (1993 trip to Africa with Melinda) transformed his philanthropic focus

**Quote:**
"When I felt restless or bored -- or got in trouble for misbehaving -- I would disappear into my room and lose myself in books or ideas, often for hours without interruption. This ability to turn idle time into deep thinking and learning became a fundamental part of who I am. It was also crucial to my success later on."

## 2. Mark Zuckerberg

**Known for:** Co-founder and CEO of Meta (Facebook), youngest self-made billionaire

**Hobbies Across Life Phases:**
- **Childhood:** Studied Latin, ancient Greek, French, and Hebrew. Deep interest in classical literature and languages. Was fascinated by the Aeneid by Virgil.
- **Teens:** Captain of the fencing team at Phillips Exeter Academy. Excelled in classics and planned to be a classics major in college. Built software projects for fun, including a music recommendation program called Synapse.

- **Later Life:** Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu and MMA training (started ~2022, now competes). Hydrofoiling and surfing in Hawaii. Pursuing a pilot's license. Barbecuing and smoking meats. Coding for fun at hackathons.

**Surprising Hobbies:**
- Was captain of his fencing team and planned to major in classics (Latin, ancient Greek)
- Quoted Virgil's Aeneid at a Facebook product conference
- Competes in BJJ tournaments and MMA fights
- Calls himself "the meat chef" -- serious about barbecue

**How Hobbies Influenced Career:**
- Fencing taught him about strategy, competition, and reading opponents
- Classical studies gave him a framework for thinking about human nature and building social systems
- Annual challenges kept him in a growth mindset and generated massive PR
- MMA/BJJ reflects his shift toward Meta's more aggressive competitive posture

**Quote:**
"I learned more from fencing, in some ways, than I learned in any of my courses at Harvard. It taught me that when you want something, you have to go out and take it."

## 6. Tom Hanks

**Known for:** Two-time Academy Award-winning actor, filmmaker, author

**Hobbies Across Life Phases:**
- **Childhood/Teens:** Got his first typewriter at age 19 -- a cheap plastic model.
- **Early Career (1978):** Took his plastic typewriter to a restorer in Cleveland who refused to repair it. The restorer showed him real steel machines, and Hanks bought a Hermes 2000 -- the cornerstone of his collection.
- **Career Peak:** Amassed nearly 250 typewriters. Created a typewriter app for Apple with authentic sounds. Appeared in the documentary "California Typewriter" (2016). Published "Uncommon Type: Some Stories" (2017) -- 17 short stories all revolving around typewriters.
- **Later Life:** Now giving away his collection, one typewriter at a time, lovingly wrapped and sent to people who will value them. Goal: reduce to just one -- the Olivetti Lettera 22 (same model displayed at MoMA).

**Surprising Hobbies:**
- Collected nearly 250 typewriters over 40+ years
- Created an iPad app that simulates different typewriter sounds
- Published a book of short fiction inspired entirely by his typewriter hobby
- Anonymously sends typewriters to antique shops across the country

**How Hobbies Influenced Career:**
- Typewriters connected him to the tactile, physical process of storytelling
- The collection inspired his debut fiction book
- His appreciation for mechanical craftsmanship mirrors his approach to acting -- valuing precision and authenticity
- Giving away his collection reflects his generous public persona

**Quote:**
"Everything you type on a typewriter sounds grand, the words forming in mini-explosions of SHOOK SHOOK SHOOK. A thank-you note resonates with the same heft as a literary masterpiece."

## 8. Haruki Murakami

**Known for:** Japanese novelist (Norwegian Wood, 1Q84, Kafka on the Shore), one of the most translated living authors

**Hobbies Across Life Phases:**
- **Childhood/Teens:** Developed an interest in jazz at age 15 after attending an Art Blakey and the Jazz Messengers concert in Kobe. Began collecting vinyl records.
- **Young Adult:** Opened "Peter Cat," a jazz bar and coffeehouse, before becoming a novelist. Amassed a record collection of over 10,000 vinyls (many now housed at the Haruki Murakami Library in Tokyo).
- **Career:** Took up marathon running in 1982 when he became a full-time novelist. Wrote "What I Talk About When I Talk About Running" (2008) -- a memoir connecting running and writing. Runs marathons and ultramarathons. Reads for one hour every afternoon and another hour before bed.
- **Later Life:** Hosted a one-off radio show discussing writing, running, and records. His record collection is now preserved at Waseda University.

**Surprising Hobbies:**
- Owned and operated a jazz bar before becoming a famous novelist
- Has run over 30 marathons and at least one 62-mile ultramarathon
- Owns 10,000+ vinyl records spanning jazz, classical, and 60s pop
- Considers himself more musician than writer -- says music shaped his prose more than books

**How Hobbies Influenced Career:**
- Jazz taught him rhythm, improvisation, and harmony -- all of which he applies to writing
- Running provides the physical discipline and mental endurance needed for novel-writing
- His music obsession permeates his novels (over 3,350 songs referenced across his works)
- He describes writing as "a very physical process" -- running and writing are inseparable

**Quote:**
"Pain is inevitable. Suffering is optional." / "Exerting yourself to the fullest within your individual limits: that's the essence of running, and a metaphor for life -- and for me, for writing as well."

## 10. Leonardo da Vinci

**Known for:** Renaissance polymath -- painter (Mona Lisa, The Last Supper), inventor, scientist, anatomist

**Hobbies Across Life Phases:**
- **Childhood/Youth:** Grew up observing nature obsessively. Believed "knowing how to see" was mankind's most important ability. Apprenticed in Andrea del Verrocchio's workshop in Florence.
- **Young Adult:** Moved to Milan primarily as a musician -- arrived at the court of Duke Ludovico Sforza with a handmade lyre decorated with a carved horsehead. Sang "divinely without any preparation."
- **Career:** Dissected over 30 human corpses as an anatomy hobby -- first to draw accurate sketches of the human spine and a fetus. Invented swimming fins at age 11. Designed parachutes, underwater breathing apparatus, armored cars, solar concentrators. Kept meticulously illustrated notebooks on painting, architecture, mechanics, and anatomy (1490-1495). Likely vegetarian for much of his life.
- **Later Life:** Interests spanned architecture, science, music, mathematics, engineering, literature, anatomy, geology, astronomy, botany, writing, history, and cartography. Owned one cookbook (Platina's "On Right Pleasure and Good Health") and was obsessed with kitchen gadgets and nutrition.

**Surprising Hobbies:**
- His primary role when he first moved to Milan was as a musician, not a painter
- Built a lyre shaped like a horse's head
- Obsessed with kitchen gadgets and nutrition -- an early "foodie"
- Likely vegetarian in the 15th century
- Invented swimming fins as an 11-year-old

**How Hobbies Influenced Career:**
- Anatomical dissection (a hobby) made his paintings anatomically revolutionary
- Musical training gave him understanding of rhythm and proportion in visual art
- Nature observation was the foundation of his scientific and artistic insights
- His refusal to specialize made him history's greatest polymath

**Quote:**
"I have from an early age abjured the use of meat, and the time will come when men such as I will look upon the murder of animals as they now look on the murder of men."

## 11. Winston Churchill

**Known for:** British Prime Minister during WWII, Nobel Prize-winning author, statesman

**Hobbies Across Life Phases:**
- **Childhood/Youth:** Avid reader and writer from a young age. Loved playing with toy soldiers (had a collection of 1,500).
- **Political Career (pre-painting):** In 1915, after being forced to resign as First Lord of the Admiralty following the Gallipoli disaster, he was in deep despair.
- **Discovery of Painting (age 40):** Took up painting during a family holiday in June 1915. Started with watercolors, quickly moved to oils. Never received formal training but pursued it with "characteristic passion."
- **Middle/Later Life:** Bought Chartwell in 1922 and spent years rebuilding it by hand. Became so accomplished at bricklaying he got an apprentice card in the bricklayers' union. Built a studio at Chartwell, laying the bricks himself. Landscaped gardens, expanded lakes, built a rockery and waterfall. Kept animals: ducks, black swans, goldfish, pigs, cats, dogs. Produced over 500 paintings in his lifetime. Wrote the essay "Painting as a Pastime" (1921-22).

**Surprising Hobbies:**
- Got a union card as an apprentice bricklayer
- Produced over 500 oil paintings despite no formal art training
- Kept pedigreed Middle White pigs and black swans
- Rebuilt his country estate largely with his own hands

**How Hobbies Influenced Career:**
- Painting saved his mental health during his darkest political moment and became his lifelong therapy against depression ("the black dog")
- Bricklaying and landscaping gave him physical release and a sense of tangible accomplishment during periods of political exile
- Animal keeping and gardening connected him to the English countryside he fought to protect
- Writing about hobbies ("Painting as a Pastime") showed his belief that creative leisure was essential to effective leadership

**Quote:**
"Happy are the painters for they shall not be lonely. Light and colour, peace and hope, will keep them company to the end, or almost to the end, of the day."

## 16. Benjamin Franklin

**Known for:** Founding Father, inventor, diplomat, printer, scientist, author

**Hobbies Across Life Phases:**
- **Childhood:** Lifelong love of swimming that began in childhood in Boston. At age 11, invented swimming fins. Discovered he could be pulled across a pond by holding a kite string while floating -- called it "the greatest pleasure imaginable." Could not remember a time when he could not read.
- **Young Adult:** Prolific reader and writer. Published Poor Richard's Almanack. Played chess obsessively (one of the earliest chess players in America).
- **Middle Life (age 42+):** Retired from printing at 42 to become a "gentleman of leisure." Spent his remaining 42 years on science and invention: lightning rod, bifocals, Franklin stove, glass armonica, flexible urinary catheter, Gulf Stream mapping. Studied number theory, geometry, statistics, and economics.
- **Later Life:** Said the glass armonica gave him "the greatest personal satisfaction" of all his inventions. Continued diplomacy and public service alongside his scientific hobbies.

**Surprising Hobbies:**
- Invented swimming fins at age 11
- Used a kite to pull himself across a pond as a child (decades before his famous lightning experiment)
- Retired at 42 to pursue hobbies full-time -- those "hobbies" became some of history's most important inventions
- Played chess so seriously he wrote an essay about it ("The Morals of Chess")

**How Hobbies Influenced Career:**
- Childhood swimming led to his invention of swim fins and lifelong love of water
- Reading and writing as hobbies became his printing career
- His "retirement hobbies" (science and invention) became his greatest legacy
- Chess trained his strategic and diplomatic thinking
- His kite experiments as a child foreshadowed his lightning research decades later

**Quote:**
"Of all my inventions, the glass armonica has given me the greatest personal satisfaction."

## 17. Frida Kahlo

**Known for:** Mexican painter, icon of self-portraiture, feminist and cultural symbol

**Hobbies Across Life Phases:**
- **Childhood:** Contracted polio at age 6. Her father encouraged her to play soccer, swim, and wrestle to aid recovery -- highly unusual for a girl in early 20th century Mexico. Became a tomboy. Boxed and swam competitively. Dreamed of becoming a doctor. Art was a side hobby nurtured by her photographer father.
- **Teens:** Was a promising pre-med student. At age 18, a horrific bus accident ended her medical aspirations and left her bedridden for months. Her mother had a special easel made so she could paint in bed. Her father lent her his oil paints and brushes.
- **Career:** Created 143 paintings including 55 self-portraits. Joined the Mexican Communist Party in 1927. Married Diego Rivera, divorced, then remarried him. Adopted the traditional Tehuana dress as her trademark. Said: "I paint myself because I am so often alone and because I am the subject I know best."
- **Later Life:** Continued painting through chronic pain. Her personal suffering became her artistic signature.

**Surprising Hobbies:**
- Played soccer, boxed, and wrestled as a child in 1910s Mexico
- Originally wanted to be a doctor, not an artist
- Painting began as therapy during bedridden recovery from her accident
- Married and divorced the same person twice

**How Hobbies Influenced Career:**
- Childhood sports gave her the resilience and fighting spirit that defined her life
- Medical/scientific interest informed her anatomically precise and psychologically penetrating self-portraits
- Painting, which started as a bedridden hobby, became one of the 20th century's most influential art careers
- Political activism shaped the content and meaning of her work
- Wrestling and boxing -- fighting against limitations -- became the metaphor of her entire artistic identity

**Quote:**
"I paint myself because I am so often alone and because I am the subject I know best." / "Painting has made my life full. Painting has replaced everything. I don't think there's anything better than work."
