## 1. Steve Jobs (1955-2011)

**Known for:** Co-founder of Apple; revolutionized personal computing, smartphones, and digital media.

### Hobby Timeline

**Childhood / Teens**
- Electronics tinkering with his adoptive father Paul Jobs, who taught him to work with tools and repair appliances in their garage
- Swimming, reading, and practical jokes
- Built electronics projects; frequented the Homebrew Computer Club as a teen

**College / Early Career (dropped out of Reed College, age 17-20s)**
- Calligraphy -- audited Robert Palladino's calligraphy course at Reed College after dropping out. This single class directly shaped Apple's approach to typography and design
- Eastern spirituality -- traveled through India in 1974 seeking enlightenment; experimented with LSD and psychedelics
- Zen Buddhism -- studied under Kobun Chino Otagawa at Haiku Zen Center in Los Altos; took midnight walks discussing life and philosophy; sat in Zen retreats at Tassajara near Big Sur
- Meditation became a daily practice throughout his life

**Peak Career (Apple era)**
- Walking meetings -- believed walking stimulated creative thinking; took colleagues on long walks around the Apple campus
- Fruitarian/extreme diets -- experimented with fruit-only diets, fasting, and restrictive eating
- Minimalist living -- kept his home famously sparse, applying his design philosophy to personal life
- Continued daily meditation practice

**Surprising/Lesser-Known Hobbies**
- Was deeply into Bob Dylan's music throughout his life
- Practiced long periods of silence and solitary reflection
- His interest in Japanese aesthetics and Zen gardens directly influenced Apple's design language

**How Hobbies Influenced His Career**
The calligraphy course gave Apple its typographic soul. Jobs said in his 2005 Stanford speech: "If I had never dropped in on that single calligraphy course in college, the Mac would have never had multiple typefaces or proportionally spaced fonts." His Zen practice drove the minimalist design philosophy that defined Apple products. Walking meetings became legendary for producing Apple's biggest ideas.

**Quote:** "If you just sit and observe, you will see how restless your mind is... over time it does calm, and when it does, there's room to hear more subtle things... Your mind just slows down, and you see a tremendous expanse in the moment."

## 2. Elon Musk (b. 1971)

**Known for:** CEO of Tesla and SpaceX; pushing boundaries of electric vehicles, space exploration, and AI.

### Hobby Timeline

**Childhood (South Africa, ages 5-17)**
- Voracious reader -- read the entire Encyclopedia Britannica by age 9; consumed up to 10 hours of books per day
- Science fiction obsession -- devoured Asimov's "Foundation" series, "The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy," and "Lord of the Rings"
- Self-taught programming -- learned to code because of his love for video games
- Sold his first video game, Blastar, for $500 at age 12
- Made small rockets and mixed chemicals (pool chlorine + brake fluid) to create explosions
- Taught himself magic tricks; once hypnotized his sister into believing she was a dog

**Teens / Early Career**
- Continued obsessive reading (sci-fi, philosophy, biographies)
- Briefly worked at a gaming startup called Rocket Science

**Peak Career (present)**
- Competitive video gaming -- plays Elden Ring, Diablo IV (reportedly ranked top 20 globally), Overwatch, Polytopia, Path of Exile 2
- Uses gaming for mental health: "If you play a tough video game, you can get into a state of flow which is very enjoyable"
- Anime fan
- Still reads extensively -- biographies, physics, AI research

**Surprising/Lesser-Known Hobbies**
- According to Grimes, he has no hobbies or relaxation methods aside from video games, but "he approaches them with such seriousness that it becomes quite intense"
- His childhood explosives experiments and magic tricks
- Comic book and fantasy novel collector as a kid

**How Hobbies Influenced His Career**
The sci-fi books (especially Asimov's Foundation and Hitchhiker's Guide) directly shaped his vision for SpaceX and Mars colonization. His childhood coding hobby became the foundation for his tech career. His reading habit -- which he maintains to this day -- is how he taught himself rocket science, famously reading textbooks cover to cover to build SpaceX.

**Quote:** "I was raised by books. Books, and then my parents." (on his childhood reading)

## 3. Taylor Swift (b. 1989)

**Known for:** Global pop/country superstar; one of the best-selling music artists of all time.

### Hobby Timeline

**Childhood (ages 0-12, Pennsylvania)**
- Horseback riding -- her mother Andrea put her in a saddle at 9 months old. By age 7 she was competing in horse shows. At 12, she told her mother: "I'm sorry, but I don't love riding as much as you do. I want to write songs and play guitar." This was the pivotal moment where she chose music
- Grew up on a Christmas tree farm in Wyomissing, PA

**Teens (Nashville years)**
- Songwriting became all-consuming
- Poetry and journaling

**Peak Career**
- Baking and cooking -- shares recipes and baking successes on social media; gives homemade baked goods as holiday gifts. Says she loves cooking because of her mother
- Painting -- uses it as a therapeutic creative outlet; shares artwork on social media occasionally
- Antique collecting -- photographed buying vintage jewelry, art, and furniture from antique shops
- Cats -- famously devoted to her three cats: Meredith Grey, Olivia Benson, and Benjamin Button (named after TV/film characters)
- Waterskiing, hiking, volleyball

**Surprising/Lesser-Known Hobbies**
- The competitive horseback riding background that she gave up for music
- She is a serious antique collector
- Painting as a private creative outlet separate from her public music

**How Hobbies Influenced Her Career**
The discipline of competitive horseback riding taught her performance and competition skills from a very young age. Her baking and gift-giving habits reflect the personal connection with fans that became her brand signature. Her storytelling instincts (visible in her journaling and songwriting from childhood) are the backbone of her career.

**Quote:** "I'm sorry, but I don't love riding as much as you do. I want to write songs and play guitar." (age 12, to her mother -- choosing her path)

## 5. Albert Einstein (1879-1955)

**Known for:** Theoretical physicist; theory of relativity; E=mc2; Nobel Prize in Physics.

### Hobby Timeline

**Childhood (Germany, ages 5-17)**
- Violin -- his mother introduced him to the instrument at age 5. He considered it a chore initially
- At age 13, he discovered Mozart and fell in love with music. The violin became a lifelong companion

**College / Early Career**
- Continued violin practice alongside physics studies
- Began sailing -- would become a lifelong passion
- Philosophical reading -- devoured David Hume, Spinoza, and Schopenhauer

**Peak Career (Princeton years)**
- Violin -- never traveled without his violin. Wednesday nights at the Einstein home in Princeton were dedicated to chamber music with friends
- Piano -- also played piano, particularly enjoying improvisation
- Sailing -- owned several sailboats throughout his life. Was famously terrible at it: got picked up by police after drifting far off course on Long Island; frequently ran his boat aground because he became absorbed in thought on the water
- His boat in Caputh, Germany was seized by the Nazis in 1933; he later owned a boat called "Tinnef" in the US
- Reading and travel journals -- kept detailed journals now held in the Albert Einstein Archives in Jerusalem

**Surprising/Lesser-Known Hobbies**
- He was a genuinely bad sailor who kept sailing anyway -- he loved it precisely because it let his mind wander
- He said if he hadn't been a physicist, he would have been a musician
- He didn't just play music; he used it actively as a problem-solving tool

**How Hobbies Influenced His Career**
Einstein's son Hans recalled: "Whenever he felt that he had come to the end of the road or into a difficult situation in his work, he would take refuge in music, and that would usually resolve all his difficulties." Einstein himself said: "The theory of relativity occurred to me by intuition, and music is the driving force behind this intuition." His sailing -- despite being terrible at it -- gave him unstructured thinking time that fed his theoretical work.

**Quote:** "Life without playing music is inconceivable for me. I live my daydreams in music. I see my life in terms of music... I get most joy in life out of music."

- Poetry -- reads poetry every evening; currently working through Mark Nepo's works

**Surprising/Lesser-Known Hobbies**
- She was giving church recitations at age 3 -- essentially performing publicly before she could read
- She reads poetry every single evening as a calming ritual

Her childhood public speaking directly launched her career -- it was a speaking contest that got her a scholarship, and a beauty pageant declaration that led to her first media job. Her reading passion became the backbone of her brand (Oprah's Book Club could make any book a bestseller). Her curiosity-driven hobby exploration became her interviewing superpower.

**Quote:** (On her Book Club) "Reading gave me hope. For me it was the open door."

## 8. Julia Child (1912-2004)

**Known for:** Celebrity chef who brought French cuisine to the American public; iconic TV personality.

### Hobby Timeline

**Childhood / College (Pasadena, ages 0-22)**
- Grew up in an affluent family with a personal cook -- but never learned to cook from them
- Aspired to be a novelist after graduating from Smith College in 1934
- Athletic and tall (6'2") -- active and energetic from youth
- Tried advertising work in New York and newspaper writing in California

**Early Career (ages 22-36) -- The "Lost Years"**
- Government jobs, secretarial work, advertising -- nothing stuck
- At age 31, joined the OSS (Office of Strategic Services -- precursor to the CIA) during WWII
- Worked her way from typist to Chief of the OSS Registry with top security clearances
- Helped develop shark repellent (tested over 100 substances; copper acetate worked best)
- Served overseas in Ceylon (Sri Lanka) and Kunming, China
- Met her husband Paul Child, a fellow OSS officer, in Ceylon in 1944

**The Transformation (ages 36-49)**
- Moved to Paris with Paul in 1948. Her first meal at La Couronne in Rouen -- oysters, sole meuniere, fine wine -- was a "culinary revelation" and an "opening up of the soul and spirit"
- Enrolled at Le Cordon Bleu in Paris in 1949; graduated in 1951
- Studied privately with master chef Max Bugnard
- Spent nearly a decade writing "Mastering the Art of French Cooking" with Simone Beck and Louisette Bertholle

**Peak Career (age 49+)**
- Published first cookbook at 49
- Got her first TV show, "The French Chef," at age 51
- Became America's most beloved cooking personality
- Continued teaching, writing, and filming until her death at 91

**Surprising/Lesser-Known Hobbies**
- She was literally a spy -- worked for the OSS during WWII with top secret clearance
- She helped invent shark repellent
- She couldn't cook at all until her mid-30s and freely admitted it
- Her pre-cooking career was a string of false starts spanning advertising, journalism, and espionage

**How Hobbies Influenced Her Career**
Julia Child is the ultimate late-bloomer story. She tried everything -- writing, advertising, espionage -- before finding cooking at 36. Her OSS experience gave her worldliness and confidence. Her writing ambitions gave her the communication skills that made her TV persona so compelling. She didn't publish until 49 or get on TV until 51, proving that your life's calling can arrive in what others would consider middle age.

**Quote:** "I was 32 when I started cooking. Up until then, I just ate."

**Also:** "Find something you're passionate about and keep tremendously interested in it."

## 9. Anthony Bourdain (1956-2018)

**Known for:** Chef, author, and TV host (No Reservations, Parts Unknown); redefined food and travel media.

### Hobby Timeline

**Childhood (New York/New Jersey)**
- Comic book obsession -- wanted to draw comics for a living; was a serious collector and fanboy of early MAD magazine
- Drawing -- submitted comic pages to magazines as a teen (got rejected on art but told his writing was good)
- Reading and literature -- parents were a music industry executive and a New York Times editor; grew up surrounded by books
- Boy Scouts of America member
- First food revelation: eating an oyster from a fisherman's boat on a family vacation in France

**Teens / Early Career**
- Punk rock -- deeply influenced by the Ramones, Dead Boys, and The Voidoids. Dedicated his book "The Nasty Bits" to "Joey, Johnny, and Dee Dee" of the Ramones
- Drugs and excess -- the punk ethos carried into decades of substance abuse
- Writing -- sent submissions to literary magazines; eventually channeled it into "Kitchen Confidential" (2000)
- Sold his entire comic book collection to buy drugs in the 1980s

**Peak Career (TV era)**
- Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu -- started at age 58 in 2014. It transformed his life. Won gold at the 2016 New York Open BJJ competition in his division
- BJJ replaced 40+ years of drugs, booze, and cigarettes: "the inevitability of getting smashed every day made alcohol a much less attractive option and cigarettes out of the question"
- Had a secret Reddit account where he posted about martial arts, made fun of Steven Seagal, and defended people with addiction issues
- Music and film -- passionate about movies, music; attended concerts regularly
- Travel as a genuine passion, not just work
- Writing remained central -- he compared jiu-jitsu to writing, saying they "appeal to the same part of his brain"

**Surprising/Lesser-Known Hobbies**
- He wanted to be a comic book artist, not a chef, as a kid
- He had a secret Reddit account for years discussing BJJ
- He won a competitive BJJ gold medal at age 60
- He compared jiu-jitsu to writing as creative processes

**How Hobbies Influenced His Career**
His literary upbringing and failed comic book dreams made him the writer who could turn "Kitchen Confidential" into a cultural phenomenon. Punk rock gave him his anti-establishment voice. BJJ gave him a late-life physical and spiritual practice that replaced decades of self-destructive habits. His curiosity -- whether about comics, punk, food, or martial arts -- was the throughline of his entire life.

**Quote:** "I am 59 years old and a Brazilian jiu-jitsu practitioner... I train as much as I can, wherever I am in the world."

## 10. Vera Wang (b. 1949)

**Known for:** Iconic fashion designer; transformed the bridal industry; started her fashion career at age 40.

### Hobby Timeline

**Childhood / Teens**
- Figure skating -- started at age 8 and became a serious competitive pairs skater
- Competed at the 1968 U.S. National Championships in Philadelphia with partner James Stuart
- Failed to qualify for the 1968 Winter Olympics in Grenoble. She was devastated

**College / Early Career (post-skating)**
- Had a "nervous breakdown" after missing the Olympics; spent a semester in Paris where she "realized I had a passion for fashion"
- Joined Vogue magazine as an editor; worked there for 17 years (youngest editor at the time)
- Left Vogue to become a design director at Ralph Lauren for 2 years

**The Pivot (age 40)**
- When planning her own wedding just before turning 40, couldn't find a wedding dress she liked
- Thought: "Maybe it's just too late for me" to start a fashion brand
- Opened the Vera Wang Bridal House in 1990 at the Carlyle Hotel in New York City

**Peak Career**
- Built a bridal empire; expanded into ready-to-wear, fragrance, jewelry, and more
- Designed Olympic figure skating costumes for Nancy Kerrigan, Michelle Kwan, Evan Lysacek, and Nathan Chen -- bringing her two passions full circle
- Inducted into the U.S. Figure Skating Hall of Fame in 2009 for costume design contributions

**Surprising/Lesser-Known Hobbies**
- She was a nationally competitive figure skater -- most people only know her as a fashion designer
- She designed Olympic skating costumes, merging her two life passions
- She spent 17 years at Vogue before ever designing anything

**How Hobbies Influenced Her Career**
Figure skating gave her an understanding of how fabric moves on the body, the drama of performance, and the emotional power of what you wear. Her inability to find her own perfect wedding dress at 39 became the founding story of a billion-dollar brand. Her skating connections let her design Olympic costumes, creating a poetic full-circle from failed Olympian to dressing Olympic champions.

**Quote:** "I thought maybe it's just too late for me." (on starting her company at 40)

**Also:** "I didn't design my first dress until I was 40, and everyone said I'd missed my window -- but starting late meant I knew exactly who I was designing for."

## 11. Richard Feynman (1918-1988)

**Known for:** Nobel Prize-winning physicist; worked on the Manhattan Project; legendary teacher and science communicator.

### Hobby Timeline

**Childhood / Teens**
- Tinkering with radios -- repaired radios as a boy in Far Rockaway, Queens
- Mathematics puzzles and problem-solving from a very young age
- Curious about everything -- took things apart compulsively

**College / Manhattan Project (ages 17-27)**
- Lock picking and safecracking -- taught himself at Los Alamos during the Manhattan Project. Used a screwdriver and bent paperclip. Terrified security officers by cracking filing cabinets containing nuclear secrets. Devoted an entire chapter of his memoir to safecracking stories

**Peak Career**
- Bongo drums -- became a serious bongo player. Gave impromptu performances at parties and gatherings. During his 1964 Cornell Messenger Lectures, he noted it was "odd" that when asked to play bongos formally, his introducers never mentioned he also did theoretical physics. His actual bongo drums were later sold at Sotheby's
- Drawing and painting -- started at age 44 (1962) after discussions about art vs. science with artist friend Jirayr "Jerry" Zorthian. Sold artwork under the pseudonym "Ofey" (a misspelling of "au fait" meaning "it is done") to avoid people buying it just because he was famous. His work was shown in galleries and he drew everything from physicist portraits to female nudes. Later learned "Ofey" was slang for "whitey" in Black communities
- Frequented a strip club where he would draw the dancers while doing physics calculations
- Traveled to Tuva (remote region of Russia) -- became obsessed with visiting the capital, Kyzyl, as an adventure quest

**Surprising/Lesser-Known Hobbies**
- The safecracking at a nuclear weapons facility is extraordinary
- He drew nude portraits under a pseudonym and showed them in galleries
- He played bongo drums so well that he was introduced at events as "bongo player" before "physicist"
- His strip club drawing sessions combined art and physics
- His Tuva obsession became a years-long quest he never completed (he died before getting a visa)

**How Hobbies Influenced His Career**
Feynman's hobbies were extensions of his scientific curiosity -- he approached drums, art, locks, and foreign countries with the same experimental rigor he brought to quantum electrodynamics. His famous Feynman Diagrams were essentially visual art applied to physics. His playfulness and hobby-driven approach to life made him the most beloved physics teacher of the 20th century and the subject of two bestselling memoirs.

**Quote:** (On being introduced as a bongo player) He noted the irony that "when called upon to play bongo drums formally, introducers never mentioned that he also did theoretical physics."

## 14. Marie Curie (1867-1934)

**Known for:** Physicist and chemist; first woman to win a Nobel Prize; only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different sciences (Physics and Chemistry).

### Hobby Timeline

**Childhood (Warsaw, Poland)**
- Intense academic focus from youth -- excelled in school under difficult political conditions (Poland under Russian occupation)
- Reading voraciously despite limited access to books

**College / Early Career (Paris)**
- Cycling -- became passionate about cycling as a young woman in Paris
- Met Pierre Curie; they bonded over their mutual love of science and cycling
- Used their wedding gift money to purchase bicycles -- their honeymoon and vacations were cycling trips

**Peak Career**
- Cycling with Pierre -- took extensive cycling tours throughout Europe: the Cevennes, the Channel coast from Le Havre to St Valery-sur-Somme, Noirmoutiers, Pouldu, Aromanches, Le Treport, and St Trojean
- The couple was "inseparable -- bicycling, traveling, and discovering elements together" until Pierre's death in a road accident in 1906
- Gardening and nature walks -- found peace in natural settings
- After Pierre's death, raised their two daughters alone while continuing groundbreaking research

**Surprising/Lesser-Known Hobbies**
- She and Pierre were serious recreational cyclists -- not just casual riders
- They used wedding money for bikes instead of anything traditional
- Their cycling trips were their primary form of relaxation and connection
- She maintained these physical hobbies despite working in conditions that were slowly killing her (radiation exposure)

**How Hobbies Influenced Her Career**
Cycling was how she and Pierre built their partnership -- both romantic and scientific. Their rides provided thinking time and relaxation between grueling lab work. The physical activity helped sustain her through decades of exhausting research in dangerous conditions. The cycling trips around France gave them shared experiences that strengthened the most productive scientific partnership of the early 20th century.

**Quote:** (Marie and Pierre used their wedding gift money to buy bicycles, telling friends they preferred this to any other gift -- their bikes became symbols of their partnership.)

## 15. Barack Obama (b. 1961)

**Known for:** 44th President of the United States; first African American president; author and constitutional law professor.

### Hobby Timeline

**Childhood / Teens (Hawaii and Indonesia)**
- Basketball -- played on the varsity team at Punahou School in Hawaii; basketball became his lifelong sport
- Reading -- grew up loving comic books, especially Spider-Man and Conan the Barbarian
- Body surfing and beach culture in Hawaii

**College / Early Career**
- Basketball pickup games -- a constant throughout his time at Occidental College, Columbia, and Harvard Law
- Writing -- published his first book "Dreams from My Father" in 1995 while teaching law
- Poker -- enjoyed regular poker games

**Peak Career (Presidency and beyond)**
- Basketball -- played pickup games regularly at the White House; famously played hoops on Election Day / caucus days to stay calm. On the morning of the Iowa caucuses, he played basketball to manage stress
- Golf -- became an avid golfer during his presidency
- Writing -- published "The Audacity of Hope" (2006) and "A Promised Land" (2020). Writing became a form of reflection and legacy-building
- Cooking -- enjoys cooking; it became a more prominent hobby post-presidency
- Reading -- reads Harry Potter, literary fiction, and a curated annual reading list he shares publicly
